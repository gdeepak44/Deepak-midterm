Einstein's_Biography

Albert Einstein, one of the most influential physicists of the 20th century, revolutionized science with his theory of relativity. Born in 1879 in Germany, Einstein's curiosity about the mysteries of the universe sparked at a young age. He challenged conventional physics with his groundbreaking equation, E=mc¬≤, which linked mass and energy, reshaping our understanding of the cosmos. His work laid the foundation for modern physics.

In 1921, Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics, not for relativity, but for his work on the photoelectric effect, which demonstrated how light particles, called photons, can eject electrons from metals.

Beyond his scientific achievements, Einstein was also a passionate advocate for peace and civil rights. He openly criticized militarism, especially during the rise of Nazi Germany, leading to his emigration to the United States in 1933.
